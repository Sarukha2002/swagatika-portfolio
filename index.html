Working Together

How I usually plug in.

Every team and platform is different, but most of my work follows a simple arc.

Start where it hurts

It starts with what hurts and what you're trying to achieve. I get close to the work — taking prototypes to real users, observing Jobs-to-be-Done in context, analyzing sessions at scale — so we agree on the problem we're really solving before anyone writes a line of code. At New Relic that meant 1,000+ sessions before prioritizing a single AI feature.

Instrument and prototype

Next, I turn that understanding into things we can react to: KPI baselines and data contracts, prototypes at the right fidelity, and controlled betas through staged rollout tooling. If we can't measure it, we're not shipping it — the metrics are designed before the feature is.

Ship, measure, multiply

Then we ship in stages, watch activation and adoption velocity against the baseline, and iterate. And I multiply as I go — mentoring PMs, writing prompt frameworks and playbooks, building the compliance and launch processes that make the next ship faster than the last.
